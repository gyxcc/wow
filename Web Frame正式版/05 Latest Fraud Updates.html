Latest Statistics on Fraud Trend

A survey was conducted in March 2024 to investigate the trend of fraud in Hong Kong and the publicâ€™s perception of fraud. Overall, 87.5% of respondents believe that they have encountered various fraud situations more frequently in the past three months than before, but about 90% of them think that they can successfully identify fraudulent calls or messages.

We have received 48 valid responses, and most of them have met the situation of receiving fraudulent calls and fraudulent text messages (including SMS or WhatsApp messages). The response also shows that the interviewees believe they might have the most possibility of encountering economic losses, as well as personal information leakage in such cases.

The survey also revealed that respondents are confident in their ability to identify fraud through a variety of signs. For fraudulent calls, most of them can be identified by the unknown phone number (83.7%), unbelievable content (69.8%), and intuition (51.2%), while it might confuse people if the fraudulent call is a local call (100%) or the caller is a real person rather than a robotic voice (80%).

In the case of fraudulent messages, most of them can be identified by the unknown number from which the message is sent (79.5%), incorrect hyperlinks to the claimed official website (70.5%), and differences in format from the official message (51.2%). On the other hand, it certainly confuses people if the fraudulent message is sent from a trusted number (100%) and if the website included in the message has a similar interface or flow to the official website (100%).

Most respondents believe they know a lot about popular scams and techniques, and they are confident in recognising scam calls, scam messages, phishing emails and scam websites among the different types of fraud. However, the lowest proportion of respondents are confident in recognising malicious applications, suggesting that they are unfamiliar with the fraudulent situation posed by malicious applications.

The respondents also provided feedback on how the Hong Kong government is facing the severe situation of fraud trend nowadays. Half of the respondents are unaware that the government has introduced different tools or measures to help the public identify fraudulent calls or messages, and almost all disagree that the government has taken sufficient measures and methods to assist and educate the public in identifying scams, as well as combating the fraud problem.
