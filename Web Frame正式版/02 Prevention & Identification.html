Prevention & Identification

It is important to be cautious and aware of the potential risks of fraud. By recognizing the signs of fraud and following the safety guides, you can prevent yourself from being a victim of fraud. Here are some tips on how to recognize the signs of fraud and how to prevent it.

Recognizing Suspicious Signs

There are several signs that can help you recognize a potential fraud. Here are some of the most common signs:

Check the message or phone call source

Check the incoming number when receiving a phone call or message which claims to be from a company. Be careful if the source differs from the one you previously received. Most (83.7% and 79.5%) interviewees can identify fraudulent messages and phone calls by the incoming number, respectively.

Check the style of the message

The use of words, tone, or writing style of the message looks significantly different from other messages you’ve received from the company. If the message contains a link, be cautious if the link in a message looks similar but does not match the correct company website URL, where 70.5% of interviewees can identify fraudulent messages through the difference in the URL.

Check the content of the message

Usually, the company does not request that you provide any confidential information, such as account passwords, one-time passwords, or credit card information. It might be a sign of fraudulent activity if the phone call or message requests your personal information.

Be cautious with over-attractive returns

When the investment return is attractive with low or no risk, you have to be careful about that. It is unlikely to have a high return with no risk. It is better to verify that the investment is licensed. Unlicensed investments with high returns might be a sign of fraud, and you might lose all your money.

Safety Guides

It is important to learn how to prevent frauds by correct way to surf on Internet. Here is some suggestions on how to prevent frauds.

Avoiding unknown links and attachments

People should be aware of unusual messages. Refrain from pressing the unknown link or downloading the unknown attachment file received from unfamiliar sources. It may lead to phishing websites or downloading malware, where the phishing website can be difficult to identify since it may have a similar interface and flow to legitimate sites (75% of interviewees who cannot identify fraudulent messages are confused by the interface of the phishing website).

The government has introduced a policy to add the "#" symbol to the sender of SMS messages sent from verified sources, such as verified banks or verified companies, to help identify fraudulent messages. Be especially careful if the sender of the SMS you receive does not include the "#". Click here to know more.

Regularly updating passwords

People should use a unique password for each account and periodically update the password. Strong passwords containing uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters are recommended to lower the risk of the password being cracked. Never use a password already exposed or labelled as a “common password”. You might click here to check the strength of your password.

Using two-factor authentication

People should verify their identity by using two or more authentication factors. Also, one-time passwords should be used. It could prevent hackers from accessing the account even if the password is already exposed.

Being cautious with personal information

People should not overshare their personal information through social media since it might be used by hackers to predict your identity and password combination. Besides, people should not share any sensitive information with anyone, such as passwords, financial information, or personal information, through the Internet or email. If a website requests personal information, input fake data to check if it verifies the information correctly as fraudulent websites won't.

Using antivirus software

People should use antivirus software, anti-spyware software, firewalls, and online phishing website checkers. It can help to detect harmful websites and malware to prevent unauthorized access to the data. People can also scan the file with secure software before opening it to ensure it is safe to use.

Using secure Wi-Fi networks

It is recommended that Wi-Fi networks be connected to trustworthy sources and password-secured networks. When using public or free Wi-Fi, be cautious not to access sensitive services, such as online banking, or online payment, since hackers within the same network might easily retrieve your information by your activity. If such activities are needed, use a trustworthy VPN to ensure the connection is protected.

Conclusion

It is important to be cautious and aware of the potential risks of fraud. By recognizing the signs of fraud and following the safety guides, you can prevent yourself from being a victim of fraud. If you suspect that you have been a victim of fraud, report it to the police and the company involved immediately. Remember, prevention is always better than cure.
