Report Fraud: Have you encountered a scam or fraud attempt? Share your experience anonymously and help alert the community. Your report can provide valuable insights that prevent others from falling victim to similar schemes.

Alex - Report Fruad
Just last week, I received an email congratulating me on winning a lottery I didn't remember entering. It included an official-looking certificate with my name and a substantial cash prize. To claim it, I had to provide my bank details and a 'small' processing fee. Thank goodness I did some digging and found out it was a common phishing scam trying to get ahold of personal financial information. Always check the authenticity before you act on such emails!
Peter - Anti-Fruad Skills
I've developed a little checklist to help guard against common scams:
1. Do Your Research: Always look up offers, companies, or individuals online before engaging.
2. Protect Personal Information: Never share personal information unless you're sure of the other party's legitimacy.
3. Double-Check Sources: If you receive a suspicious message from a 'reputable source', contact them through official channels to verify.
4. Trust Your Gut: If something feels off, it probably is. Take time to consider the situation before acting.
Remember, it's always better to be safe than sorry!
Anonymous User - Experience Sharing
Two years ago, during the pandemic, I received a call starting with an automated female voice speaking in Cantonese, "Health Department notification, we have failed to contact you multiple times, there is an important message for you, press 1 for Cantonese, 2 for Mandarin, 3 for English." This official tone got me serious, and I chose Mandarin. A man came on the line, accurately stating my name and the last digits of my ID card. He provided his employee number and the address of the Health Department, then asked if I had been to a mall far from where I live yesterday, a place where a COVID-19 outbreak had occurred, necessitating my quarantine. I panicked, hastily explaining I hadn't been there. However, he claimed my "Safe Travel" QR code was scanned at the mall. Denying any visit, he mentioned a mobile number tied to the scan which I didn't recognize. He directed me to contact a certain police officer at the Public Security Bureau.
Upon calling, I was informed that this situation, seemingly involving stolen identity information, classified me as a crime participant, potentially leading to imprisonment. Terrified, I sought the officer's assistance. He said to prove my innocence and ensure I wasn't involved in money laundering, I needed to transfer all funds in my account to a specified Public Security Bureau account, promising a swift return post verification. Frantically, I complied, transferring all 5,000 yuan I had, then inquired if this exempted me from quarantine. He said to revert to the Health Department contact, clarifying his role was solely investigatory. But then, I couldn't reach the Health Department number again, a moment that painfully enlightened me to the scam I had fallen victim to.
